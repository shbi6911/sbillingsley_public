Q9: Discuss Exoplanets¶

Q9: The fact that we are discovering planets around other stars is quite amazing, but I think it is very important for both the public and the scientific community not to grow complacent. I believe it is necessary to move beyond the transit method as soon as possible.

The transit method of detecting planets has serious limitations, in that it introduces multiple significant biases into our planetary dataset. Transit detection biases planet detection towards planets that are close to their stars, and whose orbital planes are edge-on to our own position. Having confirmed that extrasolar planets are common, the next step is to do as comprehensive a survey as possible, in order to refine our models of planetary formation and evolution, and to characterize the galactic planetary population.

How common are rocky planets vs. gas giants? How common are Jupiter and Saturn-like gas giants vs. ice giants like Uranus and Neptune? How common are rocky planets with gaseous atmospheres? How common are potentially habitable planets?

None of these questions can be answered with rigor until we can do a more comprehensive survey of nearby planets, without the biases that transit detection introduces. I've read promising things about using carefully structured sunshades to directly image planets around nearby stars, and if I had any control over space-based scientific funding, that's what I would be doing.
